# Why Organisations Hold Bitcoin

Organisations adopt Bitcoin treasury strategies for several interconnected reasons. Understanding these motivations helps assess whether a Bitcoin allocation aligns with your entity's goals.

## Inflation Hedge

Traditional cash reserves lose purchasing power over time due to inflation. Bitcoin's fixed supply of 21 million coins creates scarcity that many view as a hedge against monetary debasement. The Reserve Bank of Australia's monetary policy decisions directly impact the purchasing power of AUD-denominated reserves.

## Diversification

Bitcoin has historically shown low correlation with traditional asset classes like equities and bonds. Adding a small Bitcoin allocation to a treasury portfolio can improve risk-adjusted returns through genuine diversification — though past performance does not guarantee future results.

## Store of Value

Bitcoin's properties — scarcity, portability, divisibility, durability, and censorship resistance — give it characteristics often compared to gold. Unlike gold, Bitcoin can be transferred globally in minutes and verified independently.

## Brand Signal & Stakeholder Alignment

For some organisations, holding Bitcoin signals innovation, forward-thinking leadership, and alignment with a growing community of investors and customers who value digital asset adoption.

## Treasury Yield Enhancement

In low-interest-rate environments, traditional cash management (term deposits, money market funds) generates minimal returns. While Bitcoin is more volatile, its long-term appreciation potential can enhance overall treasury yields for organisations with appropriate risk tolerance and time horizons.

## Important Considerations

- Bitcoin is **highly volatile** — drawdowns of 50-80% have occurred historically
- The Australian dollar exchange rate adds another layer of currency risk
- Regulatory treatment in Australia continues to evolve
- Not every organisation's risk profile is suited to Bitcoin exposure
- This is **not** a recommendation — proper assessment is essential
