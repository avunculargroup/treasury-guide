# What Is a Bitcoin Treasury?

A Bitcoin treasury refers to an organisation holding Bitcoin (BTC) as part of its financial reserves, alongside or in place of traditional assets like cash, term deposits, or government bonds.

## The Global Movement

Since MicroStrategy's landmark decision in August 2020 to convert its cash reserves into Bitcoin, a growing number of organisations globally have adopted Bitcoin treasury strategies. The thesis is straightforward: holding a portion of reserves in a scarce, decentralised digital asset can serve as a long-term store of value.

Notable examples include:
- **MicroStrategy** — Holds over 200,000 BTC as its primary treasury reserve asset
- **Tesla** — Added Bitcoin to its balance sheet in 2021
- **Block (formerly Square)** — Allocated a percentage of total assets to Bitcoin
- **Numerous smaller companies** — From tech firms to mining companies worldwide

## How It Works

A Bitcoin treasury strategy typically involves:

1. **Board or governance approval** to allocate a percentage of reserves
2. **Establishing a custody solution** to securely hold the Bitcoin
3. **Acquiring Bitcoin** through an exchange or OTC desk
4. **Accounting and reporting** the holdings according to applicable standards
5. **Ongoing management** including rebalancing, security reviews, and compliance

## Key Distinction

A Bitcoin treasury is **not** the same as speculative crypto trading. It is a deliberate, policy-driven allocation of organisational reserves into Bitcoin as a long-term strategic asset. The approach requires proper governance, compliance, and risk management — especially in the Australian regulatory context.
