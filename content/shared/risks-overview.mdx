# Risks of a Bitcoin Treasury

Understanding and managing risk is fundamental to any Bitcoin treasury strategy. Each risk must be assessed in the context of your entity type, size, and regulatory obligations.

## Volatility Risk

Bitcoin is significantly more volatile than traditional treasury assets. Historical drawdowns of 50-80% from all-time highs have occurred in every market cycle. Your organisation must be prepared for:
- Material mark-to-market losses on financial statements
- Stakeholder concern during price declines
- The possibility that the investment takes years to recover

**Mitigation:** Size the allocation appropriately, use dollar-cost averaging (DCA), maintain adequate AUD liquidity buffers, and set a long investment horizon.

## Custody and Security Risk

Digital assets can be permanently lost through:
- Loss of private keys or seed phrases
- Hardware failure without adequate backups
- Social engineering or phishing attacks
- Compromised exchange or custodian

**Mitigation:** Choose an appropriate custody model, implement multi-sig where possible, maintain encrypted backups in geographically separate locations, and establish clear access protocols.

## Regulatory Risk

The Australian regulatory landscape for digital assets continues to evolve:
- ATO tax treatment could change (CGT vs trading stock classification)
- ASIC may introduce new disclosure or licensing requirements
- International regulatory developments may impact Australian policy
- Banking relationships may be affected — some institutions are cautious about crypto-related entities

**Mitigation:** Monitor regulatory developments, engage specialist advisers, maintain detailed records, and build relationships with crypto-friendly banking partners.

## Reputational Risk

Depending on your stakeholders, a Bitcoin allocation may:
- Be seen as speculative or irresponsible
- Attract media scrutiny (especially for public companies and NFPs)
- Concern conservative board members or beneficiaries
- Create perception issues with donors or grant bodies (for NFPs)

**Mitigation:** Develop a clear communication strategy, frame the allocation within a broader treasury management policy, and ensure board or governance body approval is documented.

## Liquidity Risk

While Bitcoin is highly liquid globally, converting large positions to AUD may:
- Move the market (for very large positions)
- Take time through Australian exchanges
- Be restricted during exchange outages or bank processing delays

**Mitigation:** Use reputable exchanges with deep AUD/BTC liquidity, consider OTC desks for large trades, and maintain adequate AUD reserves for operational needs.

## Accounting and Tax Complexity

Bitcoin holdings create additional compliance requirements:
- Fair value measurement at reporting dates
- CGT event tracking for every disposal
- GST considerations (Bitcoin is not subject to GST in Australia as a form of payment)
- Potential complexity with existing accounting software
- Audit requirements for certain entity types

**Mitigation:** Engage a crypto-experienced accountant, use specialist tracking software, and maintain meticulous transaction records from day one.
